How I Got Into Birding

I've always had a weird fascination with birds (especially birds of prey, and ESPECIALLY owls) since I was young, often devouring loads of nonfiction books about them in the library. However, after moving to a city, the opportunities to observe them directly became more limited, and I wasn't quite able to connect with them as much as I wanted to.

However, when I discovered the GreenerJC Urban Ecology Research Internship before the summer of my junior year (2024), I found my love rekindled--meeting amazing people like Lorraine Freeney, Alison Cucco, NJEP staff, and other nature enthusiasts opened up a world I had no idea existed! Throughout the internship, I grew to love "birding" (which is an activity I had never known even existed before), connected with organizations like JerseyCityBirds, FeministBirdClub, SMUSH Gallery, and of course the NJDEP, and learned of environmental protection campaigns I had no idea about before. I'm forever grateful to GreenerJC and JerseyCityBirds for hosting this internship and reconnecting me with my long-held, but previously dormant, passion for birds!

Random Stuff

What's a bird you have an irrational love for?

Barn owls. Barn owls all the way. Any and all barn owls really, but my absolute favorites for no particular reason are the Tyto Alba barns--something about them just absolutely captured my attention from when I was six years old, and I've continued to love them. (By the way, the Tyto Alba is recognized as having the best hearing among all owl species, and has asymmetrical ears!)

How do I get into birding?

Download Merlin and iNaturalist, and you can start with just your phone and naked eyes! Merlin listens to your surroundings and identifies birds based off calls, and iNaturalist identifies both animal and plant species by picture! As you gain experience, I'd highly recommend buying binoculars (and possibly a camera, if you're into photography), joining local birding groups, and/or taking part in citizen science projects!

What's the most underrated bird that you think deserves the most love?

Pigeons. They legitimately have a pretty sad story (read more about them here), but not too many know about it, just disregarding them as "city trash." They're highly adaptable and intelligent,and I think Rosemary Mosco really captures their unique essence in her comics! (I'm not sponsored, but I'd lowkey highly recommend her book here: "A Pocket Guide to Pigeon Watching").
